Everything Toronto homeowners need to know to plan and execute a successful bathroom renovation.

Planning Your Bathroom Renovation in Toronto

A bathroom renovation is one of the most impactful home improvements you can make — it improves daily life, increases home value, and if done properly, lasts decades. Here's everything you need to know to plan and execute a successful bathroom renovation in Toronto.

Define Your Goals First

Before talking to contractors, clarify what you want to achieve. Is this a cosmetic refresh — new tile, vanity, fixtures? A functional update — adding a shower, enlarging the space? An accessibility renovation — walk-in shower, grab bars? Or a full luxury transformation? Your goal drives the scope, which drives the budget and timeline.

Understanding Bathroom Renovation Costs

In Toronto, bathroom renovation costs range broadly: $8,000–$15,000 for a cosmetic update (new tile, fixtures, vanity, paint); $15,000–$30,000 for a mid-range gut renovation; $30,000–$60,000+ for a full luxury master ensuite. Key cost drivers are tile selection, fixture brands, whether plumbing moves, and whether electrical upgrades are required.

Layout Changes vs. Cosmetic Updates

Moving plumbing dramatically increases renovation cost. If you can achieve your goals by working within the existing plumbing layout — same toilet, sink, and shower/tub locations — you'll save significantly. Moving a toilet even 12 inches requires opening floors, relocating drain and water lines, and potentially permits.

Tile Selection

Tile is the dominant visual element in most bathrooms and the most significant material cost variable. Large-format porcelain (24x24 or larger) looks luxurious and has fewer grout lines to clean, but requires flatter substrate and more skilled installation. Mosaic tiles create texture and interest but are slower to install. Natural stone is beautiful but requires sealing and more maintenance.

The Renovation Process

A full bathroom gut renovation follows this sequence: demo, rough plumbing, rough electrical, inspection, cement board/waterproofing, tile, fixtures, vanity, accessories, final inspection. Most Toronto contractors complete this in 2–4 weeks for a standard bathroom.

Choosing a Contractor

Bathroom renovations require coordinated plumbing, electrical, and finishing trades. Hiring a general contractor who manages all trades is simpler than hiring each separately. Verify licensing for plumbers and electricians, ask for bathroom-specific references, and get a detailed written quote before signing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Bathroom services in Toronto and the GTA.

How much does a bathroom renovation cost in Toronto & GTA?

Bathroom renovations in Toronto & GTA range from $8,000–$15,000 for a cosmetic refresh to $20,000–$45,000 for a full gut-and-rebuild. High-end projects with custom tile, steam showers, and heated floors can exceed $60,000. We provide detailed written quotes broken down by scope.

How long does a bathroom renovation take in Toronto & GTA?

A cosmetic bathroom update in Toronto & GTA takes 1–2 weeks. A full renovation with plumbing and tile work takes 3–5 weeks. Projects involving moving walls or relocating plumbing can take 6–8 weeks. We provide a clear timeline before work begins.

Do I need a permit for my bathroom renovation in Toronto & GTA?

In Toronto & GTA, permits are required when you're moving plumbing, adding electrical circuits, or removing walls. Replacing fixtures in the same location, new tile, or a vanity swap typically don't require permits. We pull all required permits and coordinate inspections.

Can you move the toilet or shower to a different location?

Yes, but relocating plumbing is the biggest cost driver in bathroom renovations. Moving a toilet or shower requires breaking the concrete floor (in basements) or rerouting drain lines — adding $3,000–$10,000 to the project. We advise on cost-effective alternatives where possible.

What tile is best for bathroom floors and shower walls?

Porcelain tile is the most durable and water-resistant choice for both floors and shower walls in Toronto & GTA homes. Large-format tiles (24×24") make small bathrooms look bigger. Natural stone is beautiful but requires sealing and more maintenance.

Do you handle electrical and plumbing in bathroom renovations?

Yes. We coordinate all trades including licensed electricians and plumbers as part of our full-service renovation approach in Toronto & GTA. You deal with one contractor — we handle the entire scope and scheduling.

What is the best ventilation solution for a bathroom?

Ontario Building Code requires mechanical ventilation in all bathrooms without operable windows. We install Energy Star–rated exhaust fans sized to the room's CFM requirements. Proper ventilation prevents mould, protects drywall, and extends the life of your finishes.

Can you add a bathroom to my basement?

Yes. Basement bathroom additions in Toronto & GTA typically require breaking the concrete floor to tie into the drain system (or installing a sewage ejector pump). Costs range from $8,000–$20,000 depending on complexity. We handle permits, rough-in plumbing, and all finishes.

What's included in a full bathroom renovation?

Our full bathroom renovations in Toronto & GTA include: demolition, waterproofing, plumbing rough-in, electrical, tile installation, vanity and fixture installation, glass shower enclosures, accessories, painting, and final inspection. We handle everything from start to finish.

How do I choose between a walk-in shower and a bathtub?

If you have only one bathroom, keep the tub — it helps with resale value and is needed for bathing children. If you have a second bathroom, converting the tub to a walk-in shower is a popular upgrade in Toronto & GTA that adds usable space and a modern look.
