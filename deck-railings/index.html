Deck Railing Installation Across Toronto & GTA

aMaximum Construction installs and upgrades deck railings (guards) designed for safety, durability, and clean architectural lines. Whether you want a modern view-friendly system (glass or cable) or a classic look (wood or composite), we help you choose a railing that fits your deck and meets Ontario Building Code requirements.

Railing Systems We Install

  • Cable Railings — modern stainless cable runs with minimal visual obstruction
  • Tempered Glass Railings — frameless and semi-frameless panels for maximum sight lines
  • Aluminum Railings — low-maintenance, powder-coated, multiple colours and profiles
  • Wood Railings — traditional cedar or pressure-treated posts, rails, and balusters
  • Composite Railings — weather-resistant composite with consistent finish

Code-Compliant Guards (Ontario Building Code)

Guards are required when a walking surface is 600mm (24") or more above grade. Height, opening size, and load rules apply. We measure your deck, confirm guard requirements, and install a system that is safe and inspection-ready.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Deck railing services in Toronto and the GTA.

Do deck railings need to meet building code?

Yes. Guard height, opening size, and load rules apply when a deck is 600mm (24") or more above grade. We install systems that meet Ontario Building Code requirements.

Which railing is best: cable, glass, aluminum, or wood?

Glass and cable maximize views, aluminum is durable and low-maintenance, and wood offers a classic look. The best option depends on your style, budget, and the deck layout.

Can you replace only the railing on an existing deck?

Often, yes. We can evaluate the existing structure and upgrade the guard system without rebuilding the entire deck, as long as the framing is sound.
