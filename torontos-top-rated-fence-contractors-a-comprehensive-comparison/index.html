Toronto's Top-Rated Fence Contractors: A Comprehensive Guide

What makes a fence contractor stand out in Toronto — a guide to evaluating and comparing your options.

What Toronto's Best Fence Contractors Have in Common

After years of building fences across the GTA, aMaximum Construction has a clear view of what separates top contractors from the rest. Here's a comprehensive look at the standards that define excellence in fence installation.

Proper Post Installation

The non-negotiable foundation of any quality fence in Toronto: posts set 48"+ deep, properly concreted, allowed to cure before fence boards are attached. This single factor predicts fence longevity more than any other.

Material Quality

Top contractors use pressure-treated posts rated for ground contact (UC4B minimum), quality cedar boards for wood fences, and commercial-grade aluminum for aluminum installations. Material substitutions without client knowledge are a red flag.

Permit Knowledge

Toronto, North York, Markham, and other GTA municipalities each have fence bylaws. The best contractors know these regulations, advise clients proactively, and manage permit applications when required.

Clean Job Sites

Quality contractors remove all post hole spoil, packaging, cut-off material, and debris daily. They respect your property and your neighbours' property throughout the project.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Fence services in Toronto.

How much does fence installation cost in Toronto?

Fence installation in Toronto ranges from $30 to $100+ per linear foot depending on material and style. Wood (cedar) runs $35–$55/ft, vinyl $45–$75/ft, and aluminum $50–$100/ft. We provide detailed written quotes including materials, labour, and post installation.

Do I need a permit to install a fence in Toronto?

In Toronto and most GTA municipalities, fences over 2 metres (6.5 ft) require a building permit. Corner lot fences have additional sightline restrictions. We handle permit applications and ensure your fence meets all local bylaws.

What fence height is allowed in Toronto?

Standard residential fences in Toronto can be up to 2 metres (6.5 ft) in side and rear yards without a permit. Front yard fences are typically limited to 1 metre. Corner lots have additional restrictions. We'll advise on the maximum allowable height for your property.

What type of fence gives the most privacy?

Solid board-on-board cedar or vinyl privacy fences with no gaps provide maximum privacy. A standard 6-foot privacy fence is the most common choice in Toronto. We can add lattice toppers or increase height (with permit) for additional screening.

Wood vs vinyl fence — which is better for Toronto?

Cedar is the most popular wood choice in Toronto — naturally rot-resistant and holds stain well. Vinyl (PVC) requires no painting or staining and lasts longer, but costs 30–50% more upfront. We recommend cedar for budget-conscious clients and vinyl for those wanting minimal maintenance.

How long does fence installation take?

A standard residential fence in Toronto typically takes 1–3 days to install. Longer runs, custom gates, or complex terrain may take 4–5 days. We provide a complete timeline before starting and minimize disruption to your property.

Who owns a boundary fence between neighbours?

In Ontario, boundary fences are generally considered the shared responsibility of both property owners under the Line Fences Act. We can help you document the project properly if cost-sharing with a neighbour is involved.

Can you install gates and custom fence sections?

Yes. We install single and double swing gates, sliding gates, and arched gate tops. Custom fence sections for uneven terrain, slopes, and corners are all part of our standard installation service in Toronto.

How long will my fence last?

A well-installed cedar fence in Toronto lasts 15–20 years with regular maintenance (staining every 2–3 years). Vinyl fences last 25–40 years with minimal upkeep. Aluminum fences can last 50+ years. All our fences come with a workmanship warranty.

What is the best fence for a pool enclosure in Toronto?

Ontario Building Code requires pool enclosures to be at least 1.2 metres (4 feet) high with self-closing, self-latching gates. Aluminum picket fencing is the most common pool fence choice in Toronto — it's durable, low-maintenance, and meets code requirements.
