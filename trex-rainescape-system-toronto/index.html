Trex RainEscape System Toronto

Under-deck drainage for dry outdoor living

What is Trex RainEscape?

Trex RainEscape is an innovative under-deck drainage system that channels water away from the space beneath your elevated deck, creating a dry, usable area below. Perfect for Toronto homeowners looking to maximize outdoor space.

Benefits of RainEscape System

  • Dry Under-Deck Space - Use area below for storage, patio, or living space
  • Protects Structure - Keeps joists and beams dry, extending deck life
  • Easy Installation - Can be installed during new construction or retrofitted
  • Low Maintenance - Vinyl system won't rust or corrode
  • Warranty Coverage - Backed by Trex warranty

How It Works

The RainEscape system consists of waterproof troughs that attach to the underside of deck joists. Water that seeps between deck boards flows into these troughs and is channeled to gutters and downspouts, keeping the area below completely dry.

Toronto Climate Considerations

Toronto's weather—with heavy rainfall, snow melt, and freeze-thaw cycles—makes under-deck protection essential. RainEscape handles Canadian weather conditions effectively, preventing moisture damage and ice formation.

Installation Requirements

  • Minimum 2% slope for proper drainage
  • Deck height of at least 8 feet recommended
  • Professional installation ensures proper function
  • Gutter system to direct water away from foundation

Creating Usable Space

With RainEscape installed, your under-deck area becomes valuable real estate:

  • Covered patio with outdoor furniture
  • Dry storage for equipment and tools
  • Workshop or hobby space
  • Protected entertaining area
  • Hot tub enclosure

Cost Considerations

RainEscape adds $8-12 per square foot to deck construction costs. For a 200 sq ft deck, expect $1,600-$2,400 for materials and installation. The investment pays off in usable space and structural protection.

Alternatives to RainEscape

Other under-deck systems include TimberTech DrySpace and Zip-UP UnderDeck. RainEscape stands out for ease of installation and reliability in harsh climates.

Professional Installation

While DIY-friendly, professional installation ensures proper slope, secure attachment, and effective water management. Improper installation can lead to pooling water and system failure.
